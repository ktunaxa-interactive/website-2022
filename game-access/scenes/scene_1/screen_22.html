Greetings
Kin wam? Have you arrived? Hello! Said as a greeting to someone who is arriving.
Hu waxi. I have arrived. Hello! Said as a greeting in response to kin wam.
Kiʾsuʾk kyukyit Is it a good day? Hello! General greeting
Su'kni. It is good. Hello! Said as a greeting in response to ki'su'k kyukyit
Kiʾsuʾk wiⱡnam? Is it a good morning? Good morning! The K in the beginning of a phrase makes it a question.
wiⱡnam morning
Kiʾsuʾk kuⱡyukyit? Is it a good afternoon? Good afternoon!
kuⱡyukyit afternoon
Kiʾsuʾk kwaⱡkwayit? Is it a good evening? Good evening!
kwaⱡkwayit evening
Kiʾsuʾk ȼiⱡmiyit? Is it a good night? Good night!
ȼiⱡmiyit night
Taxta 'an wunikit! Until a little later time! Goodbye! Or: See you later!
Taxta! until, before, later Bye! Or: See you! Short form of “Taxta 'an wunikit!”
wunikit to be a long time
'an wunikit a little later (time) The 'an part makes it a "little later."
